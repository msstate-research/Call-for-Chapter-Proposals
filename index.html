Call for Chapter Proposals

The Place-based Syllabus:
Designing Contextualized Experiences for Second Language Learning

Editor: Dr. Lourdes Cardozo Gaibisso

This edited volume explores how place-based approaches can fundamentally reshape syllabus and curriculum design for second language learning by centering local contexts and environments as pedagogical resources rather than incidental and abstract settings. We invite contributions that move beyond decontextualized lists of competencies and skills in second language teaching and learning, to show how learners' immediate environments, neighborhoods, workplaces, community organizations, heritage sites, digital communities, and translocal networks, can be systematically integrated into outcomes, tasks, sequencing, materials, assessment, and second language teacher development.

We are especially interested in chapters that can illustrate how situated experiences nurture meaningful communicative and functional practices, strengthen learners' sociocultural competence and identity negotiation, and connect micro-level classroom goals to broader civic, professional, and/or multimodal literacies. Interdisciplinary work that synthesizes insights from the fields of SLA, applied and educational linguistics, curriculum design, and educational technology and offers scalable models or replicable templates for practitioners and policymakers is especially welcome.

Strands

We invite submissions of empirical studies, theoretical analyses, practice-focused reports, and design cases, that clarify principled frameworks and pragmatic implementations of place-based experiences in second-language learning aligned with one of the following strands:

Foundations of Place-Based Curriculum Design

Theoretical and conceptual chapters that define place-based constructs for second language learning, traces historical and philosophical roots, or synthesizes literature to build a coherent framework for second language learning syllabi grounded in a pedagogy of place.

Mapping Local Assets and Networks for Second Language Learning

Methodological and design-oriented chapters that describe tools and processes for identifying local linguistic, cultural, institutional, and digital assets; show how to translate asset maps into learning outcomes, scaffolded units, task sequences, and materials; and/or present templates for curriculum mapping and partnership development.

Assessment Systems that Reflect Local Relevance and Global Standards

Chapters that address formative and summative assessment strategies aligned with place-based goals, including performance-based assessment, authentic assessment rubrics, portfolio approaches, and ways to balance locally meaningful outcomes with institutional or credentialing standards.

Translocal Exchanges and Diasporic Partnerships for Second Language Learning

Studies and case reports on collaborations across local (e.g., schools, diaspora communities, virtual exchanges) that leverage translocal connections to expand learners' linguistic repertoires, intercultural competence, and civic engagement, with attention to ethics, reciprocity, and sustainability.

Media, Storytelling, and Place-Based Materials

Chapters focusing on multimodal materials design (digital storytelling, community archiving, mobile ethnography, AR/VR place-based resources) and how media practice can mediate language learning, identity work, and community voice while addressing access and issues of representation.
